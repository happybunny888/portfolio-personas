Parking Meter

On a day out, it is always important to find parking for your car while you explore!

Personas and Storyboards Project Description:
An important factor for UI is creating a design/model that can be easily used and accessible to the people who need to use it.
For this project, I observed and interviewed real users interacting with an public interface (parking meter), created two personas and illustrated one storyboard based on observations of users.



Located in Brown's Parking lot on Power Street for users to pay for their parking for a certain amount of time.
The Prompt Screen: initially displays parking rates, later prompts for parking details.
The Instruction Figures: displays instructions to users.
The Keypad: users press to select ticket type, number of hours, and confirm.
The Card/Cash Slots: users can pay by cash or card.
The Ticket Retrieval: users grab tickets to place on car dashboard.


Observations

I recorded observations of users using the meter, asked 4 users common questions, and recorded their responses.

Observations of Users Using the Parking Meter

  • Usually a single person/family uses the parking meter.
  • User often first look at the prompt screen, then at the instructions pipeline.
  • A user put their hand up as a cover/shade for the screen.
  • Often delay on selecting hours screen, sometimes cancels to retype a number.
  • Users usually pay by card.
  • Whole process of purchasing ticket less or equal to 1 min.

Questions for Users

  1. How often do you use the parking meter in Brown's parking lot?
  2. When do you use the parking meter? (During daytime on weekdays, nightime on weekdays, or weekends?)
  3. How long do you park for/how many hours do you pay for?
  4. Do you buy a day pass for parking?
  5. How do you decide how long to park for?
  6. Do you pay by cash or card?
  7. What do you do while your car is parked?
  8. How do you make sure you are back on time?
  9. How often do you add more time to your ticket?
  10. How convenient/easy is it to use this parking meter?
  11. Is there anything you would like to change about the parking meter?
  12. Have you used other parking meters in the city? How do they compare to this meter?

Summary Responses From Users

  • Usual length of parking: 3-4 hrs for Brown tour and food, or full day to visit Brown student.
  • Usually do not add time to their ticket.
  • Some set alarm for parking time.
  • Most finalize how long to park at meter.
  • Visibility issues are present: hard to see prompt screen text when sunny.
    • Hard to differentiate the yellow text when options are selected.
  • Convenient to use: prompt screen has full instructions and options, multiple modes of pay, no manually entering of license plate or card info.
  • Most have not used other parking meters in the city. Prices at this meter are more expensive.

Personas

I constructed 2 personas by creating a four-quadrant empathy map, describing what users think, feel, say, and do.
Empathy map for Persona 1:
A dad who drove his family from New York, for them to go on a Brown University tour (his son is interested in applying). He is tired from driving for a couple hours and wants some coffee, but is excited to see campus.
Empathy map for Persona 2:
A friend who drove on her own from Boston to visit her friend, who is a Brown University student. She is excited to spend the day with her friend.

Storyboard for Persona 1

I created a storyboard of persona 1's journey from start to end, reflecting their goals and all necessary steps for using the parking meter.
Possible Additions:
This Brown parking meter was used by primarily of persona 1 type, for prospective-applying students and their family. It is also very specifically different from regular street parking meters. I think it would be great if I could add a section that compares the visual design of the two meters and interview/observe people who use both.

Conclusion:
Public interfaces should be easy to use and accessible to a variety of people. Parking meters are used by many different people in the world, and use varying from either on a single occasion to a regular daily basis.
